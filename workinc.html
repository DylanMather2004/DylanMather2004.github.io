Balla Battle (2025)

About

Balla Battle is a Wave Based first person shooter inspired by Left 4 Dead and Call of Duty: Zombies, with a corporate twist. Fight your way through the hordes of corrupt co-workers, and explore the sprawling halls of the Work Inc Office. Spend your hard-earned cash on weapons at the weapon printer, and buy refreshments to get yourself through to break time!

Drink Up, Lock and Load, your Job is on the Line.

My Contributions

Enemy AI

I was responsible for developing the enemy AI. I used the Unity Navmesh package to quickly implement pathfinding around obstacles, and developed a finite state machine to handle the various enemy actions.

User Interface

I was assigned to design and implement the various UI features of Work Inc, including the HUD, with features such as a health bar, ammo counter and wave display. I also designed the main menu, pause menu and settings menu, and implemented features for each. The settings menu featured a volume slider, as well as an adjustable look sensitivity.

Sound Design

I was responsible for recording and editing all vocal sound effects in the game, including enemy hurt sounds, and the voice lines for the vending machine. All sounds were edited in Audacity, and implemented using Unity's built in audio tools.

Documentation

I authored both the Game Design Document and Technical Design Document for this project. To produce an accurate document it was crucial that I engaged in team meetings to ensure everyone's ideas were given fair assessment before completing the documents. Several drafts were written and evaluated with the team to ensure everyone was comfortable with the design and technical requirements going forward.

Admin

I was the Product Owner for this project, and was responsible for overseeing Sprint Meetings each week. Our team made use of Jira, and I was tasked with ensuring all team members were assigned appropriate tasks. I worked with the Sprint Leader to co-ordinate the group and ensure all voices were heard when making design decisions. Each member of the team was required to write their own sprint log detailing what they achieved each week. These logs were then collated into one larger sprint log during each sprint meeting to assess what needed to be done. I was also responsible for working with the technical lead to resolve any potential conflicts in the Github to ensure minimal work was lost in the event of a merge conflict.
